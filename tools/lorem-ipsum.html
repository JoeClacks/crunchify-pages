How It Works

Lorem ipsum is the most widely used placeholder text in graphic design and publishing. It originates from De Finibus Bonorum et Malorum ("On the Ends of Good and Evil"), a work by the Roman orator Cicero written in 45 BC. The scrambled text has been used as typographic filler since the 1500s, when an unknown typesetter randomized a passage to create a type specimen book. It was popularized in the 1960s with the release of Letraset sheets and again in the 1980s with desktop publishing software.

Placeholder text lets designers focus on layout and visual hierarchy without the distraction of meaningful content. Because lorem ipsum text has a natural word-length distribution similar to English, it creates a realistic impression of finished copy while remaining clearly provisional.

Classic Latin mode draws from the traditional Lorem Ipsum corpus (~200 words from Cicero). English-like mode uses common design and content vocabulary for mockups that need to read like real copy. Hipster Ipsum uses a fun alternative word list for when you want something a little more on-trend.

All text is generated client-side — nothing leaves your browser. Word counts use simple whitespace splitting. The optional seed parameter (not shown in the UI) enables reproducible output useful for consistent mockups across sessions.
